My full name is Andrés Klene Sánchez or Andres Klene-Sanchez, depending on which government you ask.

Welcome! I recently completed my MMath in Mathematics at the University of Oxford. I'm teaching A Level Mathematics and Further Mathematics at Bacon's College in London.

Broadly, I'm interested in differential geometry, (higher) category theory, mathematical physics, and their intersections. Right now I'm thinking about:

  • Smooth category theory: diffeological spaces, C^infinity rings, and their interactions via Isbell duality.
  • Derived differential geometry: higher stacks and applications to elliptic moduli problems

Work

Here's some work I've done. This includes undergrad theses, summer projects, lecture notes, and more.

Essays and dissertations

I am currently working on a set of notes called Doodles of a Mouse, after Johnstone's Sketches of an Elephant. When completed, it will be an introduction to the foundations of higher geometry, including aspects of: topos theory, infinity-categories, model categories, higher topos theory, derived geometry, and applications to mathematical physics. This project is unsupervised, but it has benefitted from various helpful discussions with Tim Hosgood, Emilio Minichiello and Dmitri Pavlov.

My Part C (master's) dissertation was on Geometric quantisation and Hamiltonian cobordism , supervised by Andrew Dancer. I wrote about quantisation of Kahler manifolds and its interaction with symplectic reduction via Hamiltonian cobordism, following the ideas of Ginzburg--Guillemin--Karshon.

Before that, I wrote a Part B Essay (bachelor's dissertation) under Guillem Cazassus. The title was Pseudo-holomorphic curves in the complex projective plane , and it is a review of Part B Essay (bachelor's thesis) supervised by Guillem Cazassus.
Topics: geometry of 4-manifolds, index theory, Gromov compactness.

Summer projects

For 10 weeks in the summer of 2023, I did a research project on symplectic topology and mirror symmetry under Jason Lotay. I was funded by the EPSRC under the vacation grant Computing Fukaya categories inductively with Fukaya-Seidel categories. Fukaya–Seidel categories and Fano HMS  [ poster ]
Topics: Triangulated A∞-categories, symplectic Lefschetz fibrations, coherent sheaves. I produced this poster (hosted at Jason's website) for an EPSRC event.

Rotational diffusions on generalised Sierpiński gaskets
Summer 2022, supervised by Ben Hambly; funded by Hertford College, Oxford.
Topics: random walks on fractals, numerical methods, harmonic analysis on graphs.

Lecture notes

I am writing the official notes for the Oxford course on commutative algebra, lectured by my college tutor Dawid Kielak. These will be based on notes by the previous lecturer Damian Rössler, with input from Gathmann's notes and various student suggestions.

Miscellaneous

I was a founding member of the TeXromancers, an online team dedicated to re-typesetting classic mathematical texts (in fact, I came up with the name!). I worked on their first two books: Stable Homotopy and Generalised Homology and Notes on Differential Geometry. Sadly, the group has since disbanded.

I also wrote an expository article called Associative Metrics for the University of Oxford's mathematics society, The Invariants.
