My full name is Andres Klene-Sanchez or Andrés Klene Sánchez, depending on which government you ask.

In 2024 I completed my MMath in Mathematics at the University of Oxford. Here's a CV.

I'm currently applying for PhDs in pure mathematics and mathematical physics. Meanwhile, I'm teaching A Level Mathematics and Further Mathematics at my old secondary school.

Current interests

I'm currently learning category theory, higher/derived geometry, and applications in theoretical physics. I'm writing some of this down in a set of notes (with working title Doodles of a Mouse, after Johnstone's Sketches of an Elephant). This project is unsupervised, but it has benefitted from various helpful discussions with many people including Tim Hosgood, Emilio Minichiello and Dmitri Pavlov.

Dissertations

My Part C (master's) dissertation was on Geometric quantisation and Hamiltonian cobordism, supervised by Andrew Dancer. I wrote about quantisation of Kähler manifolds and its interaction with symplectic reduction. Before that, I wrote a Part B (bachelor's) essay on Pseudo-holomorphic curves in the complex projective plane , under Guillem Cazassus. I studied the technical foundations of Gromov–Witten theory, applied to a simple example.

Summer projects

For 10 weeks in the summer of 2023, I did a research project on symplectic topology and homological mirror symmetry (HMS), funded by an EPSRC vacation grant (Computing Fukaya categories inductively with Fukaya-Seidel categories). My supervisor was Jason Lotay. I studied various aspects of HMS from both sides (symplectic and algebraic), including triangulated A∞-categories, symplectic Lefschetz fibrations, toric varieties and derived categories of coherent sheaves. Towards the end of the project I focused on the example of CP3 blown up at a point, producing a poster (hosted at Jason's website).

In the summer of 2022, I did a 6-week long research project at titled Rotational diffusions on generalised Sierpiński gaskets, funded by Hertford College, Oxford and supervised by Ben Hambly. I studied a class of asymmetric diffusion-type processes on plane fractals, numerically answering one of Ben's conjectures in the negative.

Miscellaneous

I was a founding member of the TeXromancers, an online team dedicated to re-typesetting classic mathematical texts (in fact, I came up with the name!). I worked on their first two books: Stable Homotopy and Generalised Homology and Notes on Differential Geometry. Sadly, the group has since disbanded.

I also wrote an expository article called Associative Metrics for the University of Oxford's mathematics society, The Invariants. To be honest, it's not very good; I agreed to write the article under the impression that I had proved a simpler answer to this MathOverflow question, which turned out to be nonsense.
