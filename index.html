Current interests

I'm currently learning about: topos theory, ∞-categories, model categories, higher topos theory, derived (differential) geometry, and quantum field theory. I'm writing some of this down in a set of notes called Doodles of a Mouse, after Johnstone's Sketches of an Elephant. This project is unsupervised, but it has benefitted from various helpful discussions with Tim Hosgood, Emilio Minichiello and Dmitri Pavlov.

There are a few projects I want to do...

  • The Isbell dual of a C∞-ring is a diffeological space, and vice versa. It would be interesting to study properties of diffeological spaces using this connection (such as Blohmann's elasticity, which was suggested to me by Emilio Minichiello). One could also explore this in the higher setting, which seems to appear in this preprint (though Isbell duality is not explicitly mentioned).

  • It would be interesting to see Morse theory done from the perspective of derived manifolds, dropping transversality assumptions. This could be applied to cobordisms where we don't ignore the singular "topology-changing slices"...

  • The "sitting instants" in Schreiber's DCCT are there to impose fibrancy on the resulting smooth n-groupoids. This is quite an artificial solution, and it would be nice to find a better one.

What I've done

Essays and dissertations

My Part C (master's) dissertation was on Geometric quantisation and Hamiltonian cobordism , supervised by Andrew Dancer. I wrote about quantisation of Kähler manifolds and its interaction with symplectic reduction. The end result is a sketch of ``quantisation commutes with reduction'' in the non-singular setting, using the technique of Hamiltonian cobordism.

Before that, I wrote a Part B Essay (bachelor's dissertation) on Pseudo-holomorphic curves in the complex projective plane , under Guillem Cazassus. I studied the technical foundations of Gromov–Witten invariants applied to a simple example, which included 4-manifold topology, index theory, and Gromov compactness.

Summer projects

For 10 weeks in the summer of 2023, I did a research project on symplectic topology and homological mirror symmetry (HMS), funded by an EPSRC vacation grant (Computing Fukaya categories inductively with Fukaya-Seidel categories). My supervisor was Jason Lotay. I studied various aspects of HMS from both sides (symplectic and algebraic), including triangulated A∞-categories, symplectic Lefschetz fibrations, toric varieties and derived categories of coherent sheaves. Towards the end of the project I focused on the example of CP3 blown up at a point, producing a poster (hosted at Jason's website).

In the summer of 2022, I did a 6-week long research project at titled Rotational diffusions on generalised Sierpiński gaskets, funded by Hertford College, Oxford and supervised by Ben Hambly. I studied a class of asymmetric diffusion-type processes on plane fractals, numerically answering one of Ben's conjectures in the negative.

Miscellaneous

I was a founding member of the TeXromancers, an online team dedicated to re-typesetting classic mathematical texts (in fact, I came up with the name!). I worked on their first two books: Stable Homotopy and Generalised Homology and Notes on Differential Geometry. Sadly, the group has since disbanded.

I also wrote an expository article called Associative Metrics for the University of Oxford's mathematics society, The Invariants. To be honest, it's not very good; I agreed to write the article under the impression that I had proved a simpler answer to this MathOverflow question, which turned out to be nonsense.
