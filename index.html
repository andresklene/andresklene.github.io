Current interests

Broadly, I'm interested in differential geometry, (higher) category theory, and applications to mathematical physics. Right now I'm learning about:

  • Smooth category theory: diffeological spaces, C^infinity rings, and their interactions via Isbell duality.

  • Derived geometry. Links to C^infinity rings, especially via Carchedi--Steffens. Interested in applications to elliptic moduli problems as in Steffens.

  • Physics/QFT. Lots of the above mathematics

I am currently working on a set of notes called Doodles of a Mouse, after Johnstone's Sketches of an Elephant. When completed, it will be an introduction to the foundations of higher geometry, including aspects of: topos theory, infinity-categories, model categories, higher topos theory, and derived (differential) geometry. This project is unsupervised, but it has benefitted from various helpful discussions with Tim Hosgood, Emilio Minichiello and Dmitri Pavlov.

Previous work

Essays and dissertations

My Part C (master's) dissertation was on Geometric quantisation and Hamiltonian cobordism , supervised by Andrew Dancer. I wrote about quantisation of Kahler manifolds and its interaction with symplectic reduction via Hamiltonian cobordism, following the ideas of Ginzburg--Guillemin--Karshon.

Before that, I wrote a Part B Essay (bachelor's dissertation) under Guillem Cazassus. The title was Pseudo-holomorphic curves in the complex projective plane , and it is a review of Part B Essay (bachelor's thesis) supervised by Guillem Cazassus.
Topics: geometry of 4-manifolds, index theory, Gromov compactness.

Summer projects

For 10 weeks in the summer of 2023, I did a research project on symplectic topology and mirror symmetry under Jason Lotay. I was funded by the EPSRC under the vacation grant Computing Fukaya categories inductively with Fukaya-Seidel categories. Fukaya–Seidel categories and Fano HMS  [ poster ]
Topics: Triangulated A∞-categories, symplectic Lefschetz fibrations, coherent sheaves. I produced this poster (hosted at Jason's website) for an EPSRC event.

Rotational diffusions on generalised Sierpiński gaskets
Summer 2022, supervised by Ben Hambly; funded by Hertford College, Oxford.
Topics: random walks on fractals, numerical methods, harmonic analysis on graphs.

Miscellaneous
