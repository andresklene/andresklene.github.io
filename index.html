My full name is Andrés Klene Sánchez or Andres Klene-Sanchez, depending on which government you ask.

Welcome! I recently completed my MMath in Mathematics at the University of Oxford. I'm currently taking a year out to teach, before (hopefully!) starting a PhD in 2025. I'm interested in higher geometry and diffeology.

Here is a list of the stuff I've written and projects I've been a part of. One day this will all get reorganised properly, but for now it's all here on one page.

Projects and dissertations

Note that both theses have received post-edits to fix typos, and in exceptional cases to provide clarity.

Aspects of higher geometry (in progress)

Geometric quantisation and Hamiltonian cobordism
Part C Dissertation (master's thesis) supervised by Andrew Dancer.
Topics: Hamiltonian group actions, virtual Hilbert representations, Hamiltonian cobordism.

Fukaya–Seidel categories and Fano HMS  [ poster ]
Summer 2023, supervised by Jason Lotay; funded by the EPSRC.
Topics: Triangulated A∞-categories, symplectic Lefschetz fibrations, coherent sheaves.

Pseudo-holomorphic curves in the complex projective plane
Part B Essay (bachelor's thesis) supervised by Guillem Cazassus.
Topics: geometry of 4-manifolds, index theory, Gromov compactness.

Rotational diffusions on generalised Sierpiński gaskets
Summer 2022, supervised by Ben Hambly; funded by Hertford College, Oxford.
Topics: random walks on fractals, numerical methods, harmonic analysis on graphs.

Lecture notes

I am writing the official notes for the Oxford course on commutative algebra, lectured by my college tutor Dawid Kielak. These will be based on notes by the previous lecturer Damian Rössler, with input from Gathmann's notes and various student suggestions.

Miscellaneous

I was a founding member of the TeXromancers, an online team dedicated to re-typesetting classic mathematical texts (in fact, I came up with the name!). I worked on their first two books: Stable Homotopy and Generalised Homology and Notes on Differential Geometry. Sadly, the group has since disbanded.

I also wrote an expository article called Associative Metrics for the University of Oxford's mathematics society, The Invariants. To be honest, it's not very good; I agreed to write the article under the impression that I had proved a simpler answer to this MathOverflow question, which turned out to be nonsense.
