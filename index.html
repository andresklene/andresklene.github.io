Current interests

I'm currently learning about: topos theory, ∞-categories, model categories, higher topos theory, derived (differential) geometry, and quantum field theory. I'm writing some of this down in a set of notes called Doodles of a Mouse, after Johnstone's Sketches of an Elephant. This project is unsupervised, but it has benefitted from various helpful discussions with Tim Hosgood, Emilio Minichiello and Dmitri Pavlov.

There are a few projects I want to do...

  • The Isbell dual of a C∞-ring is a diffeological space, and vice versa. It would be interesting to study properties of diffeological spaces using this connection (such as Blohmann's elasticity, which was suggested to me by Emilio Minichiello). One could also explore this in the higher setting, which seems to appear in this preprint (though Isbell duality is not explicitly mentioned).

  • It would be interesting to see Morse theory done from the perspective of derived manifolds, dropping transversality assumptions. This could be applied to cobordisms where we don't ignore the singular "topology-changing slices"...

  • The "sitting instants" in Schreiber's DCCT are there to impose fibrancy on the resulting smooth n-groupoids. This is quite an artificial-looking idea, and it would be nice to improve it.

Commutative algebra notes

I am writing the official lecture notes for the Oxford undergraduate course on commutative algebra, lectured by my college tutor Dawid Kielak. These will be based on notes by the previous lecturer Damian Rössler, with input from Gathmann's notes and various student suggestions.

What I've done

Essays and dissertations

My Part C (master's) dissertation was on Geometric quantisation and Hamiltonian cobordism , supervised by Andrew Dancer. I wrote about quantisation of Kahler manifolds and its interaction with symplectic reduction via Hamiltonian cobordism, following the ideas of Ginzburg--Guillemin--Karshon.

Before that, I wrote a Part B Essay (bachelor's dissertation) under Guillem Cazassus. The title was Pseudo-holomorphic curves in the complex projective plane , and it is a review of Part B Essay (bachelor's thesis) supervised by Guillem Cazassus.
Topics: geometry of 4-manifolds, index theory, Gromov compactness.

Summer projects

For 10 weeks in the summer of 2023, I did a research project on symplectic topology and mirror symmetry under Jason Lotay. I was funded by the EPSRC under the vacation grant Computing Fukaya categories inductively with Fukaya-Seidel categories. Fukaya–Seidel categories and Fano HMS  [ poster ]
Topics: Triangulated A∞-categories, symplectic Lefschetz fibrations, coherent sheaves. I produced this poster (hosted at Jason's website) for an EPSRC event.

Rotational diffusions on generalised Sierpiński gaskets
Summer 2022, supervised by Ben Hambly; funded by Hertford College, Oxford.
Topics: random walks on fractals, numerical methods, harmonic analysis on graphs.

Miscellaneous

I was a founding member of the TeXromancers, an online team dedicated to re-typesetting classic mathematical texts (in fact, I came up with the name!). I worked on their first two books: Stable Homotopy and Generalised Homology and Notes on Differential Geometry. Sadly, the group has since disbanded.

I also wrote an expository article called Associative Metrics for the University of Oxford's mathematics society, The Invariants. To be honest, it's not very good; I agreed to write the article under the impression that I had proved a simpler answer to this MathOverflow question, which turned out to be nonsense.
