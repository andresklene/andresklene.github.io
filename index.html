Mathematical interests

A couple of years ago, I was taken by symplectic geometry. So much so that, in my third year of undergrad, I wrote an essay on the technique of pseudo-holomorphic curves. I was very lucky to be supervised by Dr. Guillem Cazassus, who invited me to learn some Floer theory at the BTW3 workshop.

With the basic analytic foundations under my belt, I did a summer project on some algebraic and topological aspects of the subject; more specifically, (derived) Fukaya categories associated to Lefschetz fibrations. These objects have strong links to the homological mirror symmetry of Fano varieties, which became my focus.

This project opened my eyes to the interplay between geometry and theoretical physics. I wrote my master's dissertation on geometric quantisation in keeping with this theme. Meanwhile, I moved away from analysis and towards categorical methods; the following summer I studied higher parallel transport.

Theses

Geometric quantisation and Hamiltonian cobordism
Part C Dissertation (master's thesis) supervised by Prof. Andrew Dancer.
Topics: Lie group actions on symplectic manifolds, toric manifolds, Chern–Weil theory, Dolbeault cohomology, virtual Hilbert representations, Hamiltonian cobordism, 'quantisation commutes with reduction'.

Pseudo-holomorphic curves in the complex projective plane
Part B Essay (bachelor's thesis) supervised by Dr. Guillem Cazassus.
Topics: intersections of holomorphic curves in 4-manifolds, Banach bundles and Sobolev spaces, elliptic differential operators, Fredholm index theory, energy methods and Gromov compactness.
