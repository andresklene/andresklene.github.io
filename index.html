    Welcome! I recently completed my MMath in Mathematics at the University of Oxford. Please note that the adjacent photo is quite old.

    A couple of years ago, I got into symplectic geometry and wrote my third-year essay on pseudo-holomorphic curves. I was very lucky to be supervised by Dr. Guillem Cazassus, who invited me to learn some Floer theory at the BTW3 workshop in Marseille. Equipped with these analytic foundations, I did a summer project with Prof. Jason Lotay, where I studied (derived) Fukaya categories associated to Lefschetz fibrations and their role in the homological mirror symmetry of Fano varieties.

    This really opened my eyes to the [insert words] geometry and theoretical physics. I wrote my master's dissertation on geometric quantisation in keeping with this theme. I am currently learning some algebraic topology and higher gauge theory.

    Projects and theses

    Here is a list of the written work I completed at—and after—university. Some have received post-edits to fix typos and (in the case of summer projects) provide clarity. There is no claim to any originality of content.

    Gerbes and stuff (in progress)

    Summer project; unsupervised.
    Topics: higher parallel transport, sheaves of groupoids, ...

    Geometric quantisation and Hamiltonian cobordism [ pdf ]

    Part C Dissertation (master's thesis) supervised by Prof. Andrew Dancer.
    Topics: Lie group actions on symplectic manifolds, toric manifolds, Chern–Weil theory, Dolbeault cohomology, virtual Hilbert representations, Hamiltonian cobordism, 'quantisation commutes with reduction'.

    Fukaya–Seidel categories and Fano HMS [ pdf ] [ poster]
    Summer project supervised by Prof. Jason Lotay; funded by the EPSRC.
    Topics: Triangulated A∞-categories, symplectic Lefschetz fibrations, generalised Dehn twists, toric varieties, scheme-theoretic blowups, mutations of exceptional collections in the derived category of coherent sheaves.

    Pseudo-holomorphic curves in the complex projective plane [ pdf ]

    Part B Essay (bachelor's thesis) supervised by Dr. Guillem Cazassus.
    Topics: intersections of holomorphic curves in 4-manifolds, Banach bundles and Sobolev spaces, elliptic differential operators, Fredholm index theory, energy methods and Gromov compactness.

    Rotational diffusions on generalised Sierpiński gaskets [ pdf ]

    Summer project supervised by Prof. Ben Hambly; funded by Hertford College, Oxford.
    Topics: random walks on fractals, numerical methods, combinatorics, harmonic analysis on graphs.

    Lecture notes

    I am writing the official notes for the Oxford course on commutative algebra lectured by my college tutor Prof. Dawid Kielak. These will be based on notes by the previous lecturer Prof. Damian Rössler, with input from Gathmann's notes and various student suggestions.

    Miscellaneous

    I was a founding member of the TeXromancers, an online team dedicated to re-typesetting classic mathematical texts (in fact, I came up with the name!). I worked on their first two books: Stable Homotopy and Generalised Homology by J. F. Adams and Notes on Differential Geometry by Noel Nicks. Sadly, they are now disbanded.

    I also wrote an expository article called Associative Metrics for the University of Oxford's mathematics society, The Invariants. To be honest, it's not very good; I agreed to write the article under the impression that I had proved a simpler answer to this MathOverflow question, which turned out to be nonsense.
